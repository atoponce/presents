Types of Communication
======================

* Cell phone
* Internet
* Radio
* Television

Television
==========

* The Weather Channel
* Local News (2, 5, 13)
* National News (ABC, NBC, Fox, CNN)
* Global News (BBC, Al Jazeera)

Internet
========

* http://www.ready.gov
* http://liveatc.net
* http://weather.gov
* http://alerts.weather.gov (RSS)
* https://sslearthquake.usgs.gov/ens/
* Twitter, Facebook, other social networking sites

Cell Phone
==========

* WEA - Wireless Emergency Alerts
* E911 - location aware
* Smartphone apps (Scanner Radio, LiveATC)

WEA - Wireless Emergency Alerts
===============================

* Presidential, extreme, severe and Amber alerts
* Most phones are capable
* Provided by most wireless carriers
* Unique sound and vibration
* Will not interrupt your call
* No more than 90 characters
* Location aware
* Free text message
* Visit http://www.ctia.org/WEA

E911 - Enhanced 911
===================

* Location is associated with the caller automatically
* Both wireline and wireless is supported
* VOIP providers may require filling out a form

Radio Transmitting
==================

Unlicensd
---------

* CB - Citizens Band
* FRS - Family Radio Serice ("Walkie Talkies")
* MURS - Multi-use Radio Service

Licensed
--------

* Amateur "Ham" Radio
* GMRS - General Mobile Radio Service

CB - Citizens Band
==================

* Unlicensed
* Radios $40+
* External antennas without height restrictions
* 4 watts AM, 12 Watts SSB (about 10-15 miles)
* 40 channels in 27 Mhz

FRS - Family Radio Service
==========================

* Unlicensed "Walkie Talkies"
* Radios as cheap as $5
* Fixed antennas
* 500 milliwatts max (about 1 mile)
* 14 channels in 462 - 467 Mhz

MURS - Multi-use Radio Service
==============================

* Unlicensed
* Radios $70+
* External antennas cannot exceed 60' above ground
* 2 watts max (about 2-3 miles)
* 5 channels in 151 & 154 Mhz

Amateur Radio
=============

* Licensed - $15 per exam. Free renewal every 10 years
* Unlicensed operators may use if the licensee is in control of the station
* Three classes: Technician, General and Extra
* 1500 watts maximum (global)
* Radios $100+
* Several different bands
* 2m and 70cm popular for local communications
* Repeaters may be used

GMRS - General Mobile Radio Service
===================================

* Licensed - $85 per 5 years
* No exam required
* Shares 7 FRS channels
* 1 license covers the whole family
* Radios $50+
* External antennas cannot exceed 20' above ground
* 5 watts max (about 5-10 miles)
* Repeaters may be used
* 15 frequencies in 462-467 Mhz

Radio Receiving
===============

* NWR - National Weather Radio
* SDR - Software Defined Radio
* Shortwave Radio

NWR - National Weather Radio
============================

* Provided in many FRS and GMRS radios
* KEC78 callsign
* 162.550 Mhz
* Box Elder, Davis, Salt Lake, Utah, and Weber Counties

SDR - Software Defined Radio
============================

* $40 USB dongle with pigtail antenna
* Can receive from 23 Mhz to 2.2 Ghz
* Requires software on your computer
* Can receive FM broadcast, NWR, Amateur Radio, GMRS, etc

Shortwave Radio
===============

* $50+ radios. ~$300 for memory control, external antennas, etc
* 14 "bands" with thousands of frequencies: 2.3 Mhz - 26 Mhz
* Depends greatly on atmospheric conditions
* Great for world-wide emergencies

Shortwave Radio Emergency Broadcasts
====================================

* Federal, State & Local government agencies nationwide
* Operation Secure
* American Red Cross
* Federal Emergency Management Agency
* Air Force Global HF System
* U.S. Army Mars Emergency Network
* U.S. Navy
* Voice of America

Baofeng UV-5R
=============

* $40
* 65-108 MHz receive (FM broadcasts)
* 136-174 / 400-520 MHz transmit (FRS, GMRS, MURS, 2m and 70cm Ham)
* NWR receive (manually typed in)
* 1 watt and 4 watt output
* Dual receieve
* Memory allocation
* Antennal replacable
